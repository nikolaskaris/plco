PAUL LUSSIER

Systems solutions for a changing world

BIO



The world, as I see it, is made up of systems. It is the result of an interconnected set of elements organized in a way that achieves an outcome. A tree is a system. A forest is a system. I am a system. Systems are often embedded in larger systems, which are embedded in yet larger systems. The earthâ€™s climate is a system comprised of the subsystems of our atmosphere, oceans, land, and human society. Within the framework of systems thinking, outcomes represent the result of the interactions between various elements of a system. Systems contain within them the causes of their own failure and, importantly, their own success.

I work uniquely on problems that require systems thinking and thus have no single silver bullet solution. Rather than narrowly focusing on one approach, I take and teach a more holistic view of developing systemic solutions. At the core of my work is identifying and activating leverage points that can create significant, positive impact in climate change mitigation and energy transition for a wide range of stakeholders. These stakeholders include leading scientific institutions, governments, businesses, universities and foundations. Whether it be communities facing climate change who want to create replicable and sustainable models for others to follow, businesses that want to develop sustainable practices that leverage climate change to grow profits, or governments that want to implement energy policies congruent with the social and cultural values of their constituencies, I work across sectors to create communications frameworks that facilitate the development and deployment of symbiotic, systemic solutions to these challenges.

To develop these frameworks, a wide range of disciplines, partners and stakeholders must be brought together. To bring these various stakeholders together, communications systems and frameworks need to be developed that tap into siloed intelligences, which when brought into symbiotic dialogue with each other, enable the generation of new outcomes congruent with a habitable future.

This is the focus of my work, as an educator and researcher at the Yale School of Forestry, as director of the Yale Science Communications with Impact Network (SCWIN), and as a consultant with a broad network of global partners.
